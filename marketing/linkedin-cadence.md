# LinkedIn posting cadence (biweekly)

Goal: drive qualified recruiter/peer traffic back to `https://jassimgafoor.github.io/` via specific posts on your case studies and blog.

## Cadence

Publish 2 posts per month (every 2 weeks).

Use this rotation:

1. Post type A (Week 1): Case study highlight (problem -> approach -> measurable result) linking to `case-study.html`.
2. Post type B (Week 2): “Lesson learned” on automation/AI workflows linking to `blog.html` (or a specific case study section if you mention it).

## Post structure template

Copy/paste and fill in the brackets.

1. Hook: one sentence that describes the tension or mistake you used to see.
2. Context: 1-2 lines about the industry/team/workflow.
3. Problem: what broke or was slow/error-prone.
4. Approach: what you built (tools + why they fit).
5. Result: 2-4 outcomes (numbers if possible).
6. Takeaway: one practical takeaway recruiters/peers can reuse.
7. CTA: link to your site + a question.

## Example templates

### Template A (case study highlight)

“[Automation/AI] doesn’t fail because of models. It fails because the workflow isn’t designed for reliable inputs.”

I led a project where [brief problem]. The old way took [time] and created [issue].

What I built: [approach] using [tools].

Outcome: [measurable results].

If you want the full breakdown, see my case studies: `https://jassimgafoor.github.io/case-study.html`.

Question: what’s the biggest bottleneck you’ve seen in turning data into decisions in operations?

### Template B (“lesson learned”)

Most “automation” projects stall at the integration step because teams skip [something people forget].

Here’s the checklist I use when designing workflow automations for industrial environments:
1. Define the output contract first.
2. Model inputs as if they will be messy.
3. Build a minimal feedback loop (measure -> fix).

I’ve applied this approach across automation and analytics work. For more context and examples: `https://jassimgafoor.github.io/blog.html`.

If you’re working on similar problems, what do you measure to prove the workflow actually works?

## Linking rules (to keep results measurable)

- Always link to one of: `case-study.html` or `blog.html`.
- Mention which part of your site the reader will get (e.g. “full breakdown”, “real examples”, “templates”).
- Use the same hero positioning sentence in your first paragraph occasionally so your story stays consistent across platforms.
